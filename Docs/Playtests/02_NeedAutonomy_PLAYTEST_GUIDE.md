# Checkpoint 02 - Autonomous Need Recovery Playtest Guide

Target session: 20-30 minutes. This guide remains available for optional future evaluation; the project owner explicitly waived a human playtest for the current automated acceptance run.

## Setup

- Launch `VerifiedExtract/OpenPlan.exe` from the `02_NeedAutonomy` checkpoint folder.
- Start a New Campaign.
- Use keyboard and mouse.
- Play first at 1920x1080.
- Do not use the Unity Editor or developer controls.
- Do not drag an employee during the initial passive observation period.

## Playtest sequence

1. Confirm Morgan, Alex, and Sam begin working normally.
2. Observe the office passively for five real minutes.
3. Use 4x speed if necessary after the first minute.
4. Watch for a need entering caution or urgent status.
5. Confirm the employee communicates what they need.
6. Confirm the employee selects a logical destination.
7. Confirm the employee walks around walls and furniture.
8. Confirm the need improves after the activity.
9. Confirm the employee returns to useful work.
10. Observe at least one autonomous restroom trip.
11. Observe at least one autonomous Food, Rest, Water, or Coffee trip.
12. Wait for two workers to desire the same limited-capacity activity.
13. Confirm they do not stand inside one another or overfill it.
14. Drag a worker to ordinary valid ground.
15. Confirm they settle, evaluate their needs, and resume useful behavior.
16. Drag a worker to Rest while their needs are healthy.
17. Confirm ordinary autonomy does not immediately cancel the instruction.
18. Observe whether a critical need eventually overrides an inappropriate instruction.
19. Pause during autonomous navigation.
20. Confirm movement, need changes, decision timers, and activity timers freeze.
21. Resume and confirm the same action continues safely.
22. Earn enough money to hire another employee if practical.
23. Hire without first adding a desk.
24. Confirm the deskless worker performs phone work.
25. Confirm the phone worker leaves to satisfy a need.
26. Confirm the phone worker resumes phone work.
27. Continue passively for another five minutes.
28. Quit cleanly.

## Acceptance questions

1. Could you tell why an employee stopped working?
2. Did the selected destination make sense?
3. Did the employee recover without your help?
4. Did anyone appear stuck or confused?
5. Did anyone walk through a wall or locked area?
6. Did two employees overlap at the same activity?
7. Did workers return to work after recovery?
8. Did you understand when an action was autonomous?
9. Did you understand when an action came from your placement?
10. Did the game explain why an employee ignored or abandoned an instruction?
11. Did employees take breaks too often?
12. Did they tolerate needs for too long?
13. Did the office feel alive while you watched passively?
14. Did you still see opportunities to intervene?
15. Did intervention feel useful?
16. Did intervention feel mandatory?
17. Was any activity obviously better than every alternative?
18. Were paid food choices understandable?
19. Did a deskless employee behave correctly?
20. What was the funniest or most memorable unscripted sequence?
21. What was the most frustrating sequence?
22. Would you continue playing if employees also had distinct qualifications?

Record raw answers separately from observed behavior. Do not summarize away unexpected sequences.
