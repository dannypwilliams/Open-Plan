# Endless Office Alpha — Checkpoint 00 Foundation Playtest

- Build: `00_Foundation`
- Suggested session: 15–20 minutes
Executable: `OpenPlan.exe`

## What to do

1. Start a new Starter Office campaign and confirm the HUD begins at `$0` with `Team 3` and `Desks 3`.
2. Try one wheel notch, then zoom between the full overview and a close worker view.
3. Pick up a worker and move across ordinary floor, activity landmarks, walls, and the locked neighboring unit. Read the cursor label before releasing.
4. Release once on ordinary valid floor and watch the worker pause briefly, resume autonomy, and return to useful behavior.
5. Release near Water, Rest, Vending, Smoking, Work, or Leave Office without aiming only at the visible footprint.
6. Try invalid walls, occupied desks, and locked neighboring space. Confirm the worker returns safely and is never left elevated.
7. Inspect the water cooler and smoking alcove from overview and close camera distances.
8. Let the original team earn real cash. Open Hiring before buying the neighboring unit and hire the cheapest affordable candidate.
9. Confirm the fourth employee has no desk, visibly works from a phone, earns more slowly, can take breaks, and stops earning while paused.
10. Resume, continue managing freely, return to the main menu, and quit.

Please do not buy the neighboring unit until after trying pre-expansion hiring. There is no need to exhaust every activity; spend the rest of the session following whatever seems interesting or confusing.

## Questions

1. Did one mouse-wheel notch produce a noticeable zoom change?
2. Could you zoom from overview to close view without excessive scrolling?
3. Was it obvious that a worker could be dropped on ordinary office floor?
4. Could you tell when a nearby activity would influence the drop?
5. Were invalid walls, locked space, and unavailable destinations explained clearly?
6. Did the office feel denser and more visually complete than the previous tutorial build?
7. Was the smoking area readable rather than black or unfinished?
8. Did the water cooler read as an important activity landmark?
9. Did starting at `$0` make the first earnings feel meaningful?
10. Was it clear that hiring was available before expansion?
11. Could you recognize that a deskless employee was working from a phone?
12. Did the phone employee appear less productive than a desk employee?
13. Did anything become stuck, remain elevated, stop earning unexpectedly, or disappear?
14. What was the first confusing moment?
15. What single change would most improve this foundation?
